Product design · UX research · 0→1

A live shelf data tool that saved sales teams 600+ hours a year.

Company
Kraft Heinz
Role
Staff Product Designer
Duration
2023 – Present
Tools
Figma · React · Miro
I owned — design strategy, UX, visual design, prototyping, research practice / Collaborated with — product owner, engineering, end users
The problem

Two-week-old data. No way to act in time.

Retail teams at Kraft Heinz were relying on post-point-of-sale analytics that lagged by up to two weeks, making it nearly impossible to take timely action on shelf conditions, pricing, or promotions. There was no real-time visibility tool, just spreadsheets and delayed reports.

I led Virtual Store Walk from proof of concept to a scaled, React-based web application, owning all design strategy, UX, and visual design across multiple MVP iterations. To make sure each iteration was solving the right problems, I introduced a new approach to product reviews, running quarterly workshops with 15 key users in Miro to pressure-test assumptions, surface real needs, and shape roadmap priorities.

Process

From proof of concept to scaled product

Phase one

Proof of concept

Early research defined the core problem. Initial POC validated that real-time shelf visibility was worth building.

Phase two

MVP iterations

Quarterly Miro workshops with 15 key users shaped each iteration. Continuous discovery replaced assumption-driven design.

Phase three

Scaled product

Multiple MVP cycles led to a polished, React-based tool adopted by global sales teams across the organization.

Early MVP (left) vs. final product (right) — filtered views, streamlined interface, and new components added over multiple iterations.

Early MVP filtering (left) vs. current state (right) — showing the evolution of the filtering experience across iterations.

Visioning & strategy · B2C & B2B · Executive engagement

Redefining the future of moving for a Fortune 500 company.

The problem

Financially strong, held back by analog systems.

This Fortune 500 moving and storage company was at a pivotal moment, growing revenue, but constrained by outdated systems and a customer experience that felt stressful and fragmented. The opportunity extended beyond improving the core moving experience to exploring new revenue streams, strategic partnerships, and a vision for becoming a trusted partner for life's transitions.

Over 8 weeks, I led design strategy and visual design across both B2C and B2B experiences, contributed to research alongside the research team, and collaborated with solution engineering on the B2B side. The output was a suite of proof-of-concept screens and a strategic narrative — designed to earn executive buy-in and serve as the blueprint that would directly inform the final product build if the client moved forward with Salesforce.

Rapid prototyping · Sales strategy · Figma

A Las Vegas card game that became a reusable sales asset.

Company
Salesforce
Role
Lead Designer
Duration

Tools

The bigger vision behind the prototype: a unified experience platform connecting guest personalization with team member tools.

The problem

One week to win a meeting, and build something that scales.

A meeting with a top resort casino executive was already on the calendar, and the sales team needed something that would stand out. But the goal wasn't just to win one meeting, it was to build something reusable that could flex across the entire travel and hospitality vertical, without being locked to any one brand.

I partnered with the travel and hospitality sales team to design a Las Vegas-style card game prototype in Figma, a tool I was using for the first time. To create the card-flip interaction, I adapted techniques from a mobile payment tutorial, learning enough Figma in days to ship something polished under a hard deadline.
